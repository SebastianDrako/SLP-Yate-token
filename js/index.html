SLP paper wallets: quick, easy, useful and versatile

Paper wallets are a fast and convenient way to store Simpleledger Protocol (SLP) tokens offline. You don't need a different wallet for every new token: One single paperwallet can hold ALL kinds of different SLP tokens, or you can make individual wallets for each SLP token if you prefer -- the technology supports both options and how you use it is entirely up to you.

You can even spend or gift your SLP tokens without ever connecting to the internet by treating a paper wallet as cash. Paper wallets are a physical way to store and spend any kind of SLP token. Store your paper wallet in a safe location where only you can access.

Benefits

Cold Storage

Paper wallets allow you to store SLP tokens completely offline. For best security, we recommend downloading the source code and generating the seed from an offline laptop.

Easy To Create

Anyone can create a paper wallet! You only need a printer, scissors, and tape.

Convenient Spending

Spend by scanning the paper wallet’s private key with a compatible SLP-aware wallet, or like physical cash (great for gift cards or store handouts).

How it works

1

Use the client-side wallet generator to create a unique private key and simpleledger:address


2

Print out the wallet on a secure printer.


3

Cut along the border, then fold the private key section over. Use tape to seal the private key section from view.


4

Send any amount of Simpleledger (SLP) tokens to the public address. Your new wallet is compatible with ALL simpleledger tokens.


5

Keep the wallet in a safe place, such as a safe deposit box or a room/space in your home you have exclusive access to, or you can even give it as a gift.


6

When you want to spend or move your funds, simply reveal the private key and enter it into a compatible SLP-aware BCH wallet. (Mobile SLP wallets with sweep/scan features for SLP are under development to make it easier to claim the tokens from your paper wallet.)

Seed Generator Security

The wallet seed generator is based on the extensively tested Bitaddress.org, which lets you generate a paper wallet in a fast and secure way. The generator code is open source and peer-reviewed. It uses your browser to generate the wallet, and not our servers. Nothing leaves your browser and if you prefer, you can even download and run this code entirely locally on an offline computer.

Extra Security Tips

Our paper wallet is generated using a browser-side random address generator. This means the wallet is created in your browser. No data is transmitted to our servers or across the web. Generally, it is acceptably safe to use as is. However, if you are using the wallet for larger amounts or just want to ensure extra security please take these tips into consideration.

Run Offline

Running the generator off a live site presents bad actors the potential to intercept your paper wallet's private key. It's not likely, but to mitigate the risk we highly recommend downloading the page files from our site first. Then turn off your internet connection and run the page locally. Just open the downloaded zip and open the index.html file in your web browser. Click to Download Generator

Clean Operating System

Make sure you are using a trusted computer free from any viruses or malware. Do not create your wallet in view of any prying eyes or cameras.

Safe Printer

Again make sure you are using a trusted printer. Do not wirelessly connect with the printer. Use a direct connection instead, and make sure no data is saved in your print queue or on the printer afterwards.

Don't Save

Do not save your paper wallet as an image or digital file. Paper cannot be hacked, your computer can be.

Treat it like Cash

Once your wallet is made and you have sent funds to it, it is essentially like cash. Whoever physically holds it can access the funds. It can be lost, stolen, or destroyed and there is no backup. Take whatever precautions necessary to ensure it stays safe until needed.

How to Use Our Paper Wallet

How do I keep it safe?

You don’t want anyone to access your SLP tokens, that’s why you have to keep your paper wallet in a safe place. You should choose a place in your home that you exclusively have access to. This can be a safe deposit box, a hidden place in your room, a cabinet, or even a specific page of your favorite book. It's good practice to keep your paper wallet in a watertight bag (e.g. Zip-Loc) or a box where water or other liquids can’t damage it.

How do I load funds to a paper wallet?

Use any software wallet with SLP tokens to send any amount of them to your paper wallet. You, or anyone else, can deposit funds to the paper wallet by scanning the wallet’s public address and sending any kind of SLP token (in any amount) to it.

How do I verify how much is on it?

You can verify your Simpleledger SLP token balance by using block explorers like simpleledger.info or explorer.bitcoin.com/bch/tokens and entering the public address on your paper wallet, either by copy-pasting or typing in your public key to the search bar. Because the funds reside on the blockchain, paper wallet balances are updated in real time.

How do I withdraw funds?

To withdraw funds from your paper wallet, you need a “normal” SLP-aware wallet (either an online, exchange or software wallet), which you will use to load your paper wallet. Software wallets which allow you to "sweep" (scan) the private key of a paper wallet are in development, but you can also input or copy/paste your private key into a wallet like Electron Cash SLP edition to claim your funds. For security reasons, if you withdraw funds from your paper wallet, you should withdraw ALL of your balance at once. Don’t leave any funds in your paper wallet, as it could get lost. NEVER use the same paper wallet to deposit funds again. Just create a new one instead.
